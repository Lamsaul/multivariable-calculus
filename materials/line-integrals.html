Line Integrals

How Does the Computation Work?

For a path along a surface,

\[ \int_C f(x,y) \cdot ds = \int_{t=a}^{t=b} f(x(t),y(t)) ||r'(t)|| \cdot dt \]

Here, we're integrating along a path rather than two given limits.

For a vector field \(F(x,y,z)=F(x(t), y(t), z(t))\) and \(C: r(t)=x(t)\hat{i}+y(t)\hat{j}+z(t)\hat{k}\) for \(b \leq t \leq a\), the work done for a point to move along the curve is,

\[ w=\int_{t=a}^{t=b} F(\vec{r}(t)) \cdot r'(t) \cdot dt = \int_C F \cdot dr \]

Examples

  1. \[ \int_C xyz^2 \cdot ds \]

    \(C\): The segment \((1,1,0) \to (2,3,1)\)

    Set up a parametric equation:

    \[ x(t) = x_o + k_1t = 1 + k_1t \]

    \[ y(t) = y_o + k_2t = 1 + k_2t \]

    \[ z(t) = z_o + k_3t = 0 + k_3t \]

    At \((2,3,1)\) and \(t=1\),

    \(2 = 1 + k_1 \Rightarrow k_1=1\)

    \(3=1+k_2 \Rightarrow k_2 = 2\)

    \(k_3=1\)

    So, we have:

    \[ x(t)=1+t \]

    \[ y(t)=1+2t \]

    \[ z(t)=t \]

    This gives us \(0 \leq t \leq 1\) for this particular set of parametric equations. And,

    \[ \vec{r}(t) = x\hat{i}+y\hat{j}+z\hat{k} = (1+t)\hat{i} + (1+2t)\hat{j} + t\hat{k} \]

    \[ r'(t) = \hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + \hat{k} \]

    \[ ||r'(t)|| = \sqrt{6} \]

    \[ \int_C xyz^2 \cdot ds = \sqrt{6}\int_{t=0}^{t=1} (1+t)(2+t)t \cdot dt = \frac{89\sqrt{6}}{60} \]

  2. \[ \int_C xy \cdot dx + (x+y)\cdot dy \]

    Two segments: \(C_1:(1,2) \to (3,4)\) and \(C_2:(3,4) \to (4,0)\)

    For \(C_1\): \(0\leq t \leq 1\),

    \[ x=1+2t \]

    \[ y=2+2t \]

    For \(C_2\): \(0\leq t \leq 1\),

    \[ x=3+t \]

    \[ y=4-4t \]

    For \(C_1\): \(dx=2dt\) and \(dy=2dt\),

    \[ 8\int_{t=0}^{t=1} (1+2t)(1+t) \cdot dt + (3+4t)\cdot dt = \frac{68}{3} \]

    For \(C_2\): \(dx=dt\) and \(dy=-4dt\)

    \[ \int_{t=0}^{t=1} (3+t)(4-4t) \cdot dt (7-3t) \cdot -4dt = -\frac{46}{3} \]

    Adding them up, we get:

    \[ \frac{22}{3} \]

Example: Conservative Vector Fields

  1. Calculate the work done on \(F(x,y)=(2y+1)\hat{i}+(2x+3)\hat{j}\) from \(A(0,0)\) to \(B(-1,1)\)

    1. Determine if \(F\) is conservative:

      \[ \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial P}{\partial y} = 2 \]

      Thus, \(F\) is conservative.

    2. Finding \(f\) for \(F= \nabla f\).

      If \(\nabla f=F\), then,

      \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 2y+1 \]

      \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 2x+3 \]

      \[ f(x,y)=\int \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \cdot dx = \int 2y+1 \cdot dx = 2xy+x+g(y) \]

      We add some function \(g(y)\) as a replacement for the usual constant because during integration we treat \(y\) as a constant. We can now get another expression for the partial derivative of \(f\) with respect to \(y\),

      \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 2x+g'(y) \]

      Equating this to our original expression,

      \[ 2x+g'(y)=2x+3 \Rightarrow g'(y) = 3 \]

      We can get \(g(y)\) as,

      \[ g(y) = \int g'(y) \cdot dy = 3y+c \]

      Thus,

      \[ f(x,y) = 2xy+x+3y+c \]

    3. Calculating the work done,

      \[ \int_C F \cdot dr = f(b)-f(a) = f(-1,1)-f(0,0) = (-2-1+3+c)-(0+c) = 0 \]

Green's Theorem

The Result

  • For a non-conservative vector field, work done is
    • \[\oint_C \left( P \, dx + Q \, dy \right) = \iint_D \left( \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial P}{\partial y} \right) \, dA\] Flux is given by, \[\oint_C \vec{F} \cdot \hat{n} \cdot ds = \int\int_R (\nabla \cdot \vec{F}) \cdot dA\]

Examples

  1. \[ \oint_C x^3 \cdot dx + xy \cdot dy \]

    For \(C: (0,0), (1,1), (0,1)\).

    \[ \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} = y \]

    \[ \frac{\partial P}{\partial y} = 0 \]

    Our region is defined between the lines \(y=x\), \(y=1\), \(y=0\), and \(x=0\).

    \[ \int_{y=0}^{y=1} \int_{x=0}^{x=y} y^2 \cdot dx \cdot dy = \int_{y=0}^{y=1} \frac{1}{3}y^3 \cdot dy = \frac{1}{3} \]

  2. \[ \oint_C (y^2 + \cos(x)) \cdot dx + (x-\arctan(y)) \cdot dy \]

    For \(C: y=4-x^2\) and \(y=0\).

    \[ \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} = 1 \]

    \[ \frac{\partial P}{\partial y} = 2y \]

    \[ \int_R \int 1-2y \cdot dy \cdot dx = \int_{x=-2}^{x=2} \int_{y=0}^{y=4-x^2} 1-2y \cdot dy \cdot dx \]

    \[ -\frac{352}{15} \]
