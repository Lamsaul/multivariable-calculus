1st and 2nd Moments

  • For a density function \(\rho(x,y)\), mass \(m\), center of mass position \((\bar{x}, \bar{y})\) of a 2-D lamina,

    \[m=\int_R\int \rho(x,y) \cdot dA\]

  • The moments of mass \(M_x\) and \(M_y\) are given by,

    \[M_x = \int_R \int y\cdot \rho(x,y) \cdot dA\]
    \[M_y=\int_R \int x \cdot \rho(x,y) \cdot dA\]
    \[\bar{x} = \frac{M_y}{m}, \bar{y}=\frac{M_x}{m}\]

  • And the second moments of inertia are given by,

    \[I_x = \int_R \int y^2\cdot \rho(x,y) \cdot dA\]
    \[I_y=\int_R \int x^2 \cdot \rho(x,y) \cdot dA\]

  • About the origin, \(I_o = I_x + I_y\).

Introduction to Triple Integrals

\[\int f(x) \cdot dx\]

  1. Area under \(f(x)\) (2-D)
  2. Mass of a straight wire (1-D)

\[\int_R\int f(x,y) \cdot dA\]

  1. Volume under \(f(x,y)\) (3-D)
  2. Mass of a lamina (2-D)

\[\int \int \int f(x,y,z) \cdot dV\]

  1. Some measurement of region on \(f(x,y,z)\) (4-D)
  2. Mass of an object (3-D).

    3. The mass idea only works if \(f(x)\), \(f(x,y)\) and \(f(x,y,z)\) are density functions.
