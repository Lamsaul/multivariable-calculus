Cylindrical Coordinates

  • Polar coordinates with a \(z\) component.
  • \(Pol(r, \theta) \rightarrow Cyl(r, \theta, z)\)

    \[ \begin{matrix} x = r\cos{\theta} \\ y=r\sin{\theta} \\ z = z \end{matrix} \]

  • Where \(r=x^2+y^2\).
  • Note that \(r\) is NOT the distance from the origin to a point. It's the distance from the origin to the projection of a point to the X-Y plane.

Spherical Coordinates

  • Uses distance from the origin to the point, denoted as \(\rho\).
  • Two angles are considered: \(\theta\) (just like cylindrical) and \(\phi\) (angle from the positive \(z\) axis).
  • For \(Sph(\rho, \theta, \phi)\),

    \[ \begin{matrix} \rho \geq 0 \\ 0 \leq \theta \leq 2 \pi \\ 0 \leq \phi \leq \pi \end{matrix} \]

  • And,

    \[ \begin{matrix} r = \rho\sin{\phi} \\ x = r\cos{\theta} = \rho\cos{\theta}\sin{\phi}\\ y = r\sin{\theta}=\rho\sin{\theta}\sin{\phi}\\ z = \rho\cos{\phi} \\ \rho = x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \\ \tan{\theta} = \frac{y}{x} \\ \cos{\phi} = \frac{z}{\rho} \end{matrix} \]

Triple Integrals in Cylindrical/Spherical Coordinates

  • For cylindrical coordinates \(f(x,y,z)=f(r\cos{\theta}, r\sin{\theta}, z)\),

    \[ \int \int\int f(x,y,z) \cdot dV \to \int_{\theta_1}^{\theta_2}\int_{r_1}^{r_2}\int_{z_1}^{z_2}f(r\cos{\theta}, r\sin{\theta},z) r \cdot dz \cdot dr \cdot d\theta \]

  • Spherical coordinates,

    \[ \int\int\int f(x,y,z) \cdot dV = \int_{\theta_1}^{\theta_2} \int_{\phi_1}^{\phi_2} \int_{\rho_1}^{\rho_2} f(x,y,z) \rho^2 \cdot \sin{\phi} \cdot d\rho \cdot d\phi \cdot d\theta \]
