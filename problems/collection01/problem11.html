Problem 11

  1. Evaluate the surface integral \(\int \int_{\sum} \vec{F} \cdot \hat{n} \cdot dS\) where \(\sum\) is the part of the cone \(z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) beneath the plane \(z=1\) with downward orientation and \(\vec{F}=x\hat{i}+y\hat{j}+z^4\hat{k}\).

Solution

\[\int\int_S \vec{F} \cdot \hat{n} \cdot dS = \int\int_R (-Pg_x - Qg_y + M) \cdot dA\] Here, \[P=x\\ Q=y\\ M=z^4 \\ g_x = \frac{\partial\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}{\partial x} = \frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}} \\ g_y = \frac{\partial \sqrt{x^2+y^2}}{\partial y} = \frac{y}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}\] \[\int\int_S \vec{F} \cdot \hat{n} \cdot dS = \int\int_R -\frac{x^2}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}-\frac{y^2}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}+(x^2+y^2)^2 \cdot dA\] Using polar coordinates, \[\int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_0^1 r\left(-\frac{r^2}{r} + r^4\right) drd\theta = \int_0^{2\pi} \left[-\frac{r^3}{3}+\frac{r^6}{6}\right]_0^1 \cdot d\theta = \int_0^{2\pi} -\frac{1}{6} \cdot d\theta = -\frac{\pi}{3}\]
