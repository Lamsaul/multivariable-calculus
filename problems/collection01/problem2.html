Problem 2

  1. Show that the average distance of the points of a disk of radius a to its center is \(2a/3\).

Solution

The first moment of area can be determined as, \[Q=\int_A r \cdot dA\] We know that, $dA= rdrd\theta$, \[Q=\int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^a r \cdot r \cdot drd\theta=\int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^a r^2 \cdot drd\theta\] \[\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{a^3}{3} \cdot d\theta = \frac{2\pi a^3 }{3}\] We know that the total area of the disk is: \[\pi a^2\] The mean distance of all points from the center is hence, \[\bar{r} = \frac{\frac{2\pi a^3}{3}}{\pi a^2} = \frac{2}{3}a\]
