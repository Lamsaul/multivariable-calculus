Problem 5

  1. Find the area of the ellipse, \[(2x+5y-3)^2 + (3x-7y+8)^2 = 1\]

Solution

Let \(u=2x+5y-3\) and \(v=3x-7y+8\), We've transformed it into a unit circle in the $uv$ plane. Its area in the \(uv\) plane is \(\pi\), and it can more generally be expressed as: \[\int\int_R 1 \cdot du \cdot dv\] If we want to measure it in the XY plane, \[\text{Area}(xy) = \text{Area}(uv) \cdot J(x,y) = \frac{\text{Area}(uv)}{J(u,v)}\] Since it'll be more convenient to calculate \(J(u,v)\), \[J(u,v) = \begin{vmatrix} \frac{\partial (2x+5y-3)}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial (2x+5y-3)}{\partial y} \\ \frac{\partial (3x-7y+8)}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial (3x-7y+8)}{\partial y} \end{vmatrix} = \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ 3 & -7 \end{vmatrix} = -29\] Thus, we have, \[\text{Area}(xy) = \pi \cdot \frac{1}{|-29|} = \frac{\pi}{29}\]
