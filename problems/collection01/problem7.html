Problem 7

Consider the vector field

\[\vec{F} = \frac{-y\hat{i}+x\hat{j}}{x^2+y^2}\]
  1. Show that \(\vec{F}\) is the gradient of the polar function \(\theta(x,y) = \arctan(y/x)\) over the right half-plane \(x > 0\).
  2. Suppose that \(C\) is a smooth curve in the right half-plane \(x > 0\) joining two points \(A : (x_1 , y_1 )\) and \(B : (x_2 , y_2 )\). Express \(\int_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}\) in terms of the polar coordinates \((r_1, \theta_1)\) and \((r_2, \theta_2)\). of \(A\) and \(B\).
  3. Compute directly from the deÔ¨Ånition the line integrals \(\int_{C_1} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}\) and \(\int_{C_2} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}\) where \(C_1\) is the upper half of the unit circle running from \((1,0)\) to \((-1, 0)\) and \(C_2\) is the lower half of the unit circle, also going from \((1,0)\) to \((-1, 0)\).

Solution

  1. \[\nabla \theta = \frac{\partial \left(\arctan{y/x}\right)}{\partial x} \hat{i} + \frac{\partial \left(\arctan{y/x}\right)}{\partial y} \hat{j}\] \[\frac{\partial \left(\arctan{y/x}\right)}{\partial x} = \frac{d\arctan(y/x)}{d(y/x)} \cdot \frac{d(y/x)}{dx} = -\frac{y}{x^2+y^2}\] \[\frac{\partial \left(\arctan{y/x}\right)}{\partial y} = \frac{d\arctan(y/x)}{d(y/x)} \cdot \frac{d(y/x)}{dy} = \frac{x}{x^2+y^2}\]

  2. Is \(\vec{F}\) conservative?

    \[\frac{d\left(-\frac{y}{x^2+y^2}\right)}{dy} = -\frac{(x^2+y^2) - 2y^2}{(x^2+y^2)^2} = \frac{y^2-x^2}{x^2+y^2}\] \[\frac{d\left(\frac{x}{x^2+y^2}\right)}{dx} = \frac{x^2+y^2 - 2x^2}{x^2+y^2} = \frac{y^2-x^2}{x^2+y^2}\]

    Yes it is!

    So, \[\int_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = \theta(r_2, \theta_2) - \theta(r_1, \theta_1) = \theta_2 - \theta_1\]
  3. On the upper half, at \((1,0)\), \(\theta_1 = 0\) and \(\theta_2 = \pi\), so \[\int_{C_1} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = \pi\] And on the lower half, at \((1,0)\), \(\theta_1 = 0\) and \(\theta_2 = -\pi\), \[\int_{C_2} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = -\pi\]
