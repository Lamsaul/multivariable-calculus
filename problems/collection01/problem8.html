Problem 8

  1. Calculate the curl of \(\vec{F} = r^n (x \hat{i} + y \hat{j})\).
  2. Whenever possible, Ô¨Ånd a potential \(g\) such that \(\vec{F}= \nabla g\)

Solution

  1. \[Curl(\vec{F}) = \frac{d(x(x^2+y^2)^{n/2})}{dy} - \frac{d(y(x^2+y^2)^{n/2})}{dx}\] \[\frac{d(x(x^2+y^2)^{n/2})}{dy}=(x^2+y^2)^{n/2}\frac{dx}{dy} + x \frac{d(x^2+y^2)^{n/2}}{d(x^2+y^2)} \cdot \frac{d(x^2+y^2)}{dy} = x\frac{n}{2} \frac{(x^2+y^2)^{n-2}}{r} \cdot 2y = 2nxy \frac{(x^2+y^2)^{n-2}}{r} \] \[\frac{d(y(x^2+y^2)^{n/2})}{dx} = y \frac{(dx^2+y^2)^{n/2}}{d(x^2+y^2)} \cdot \frac{d(x^2+y^2)}{dx} = 2nxy \frac{(x^2+y^2)^{n-2}}{r}\] Thus, the curl is 0.
  2. If \(g=g(r)\), \[\nabla g = g'(r) \frac{x}{r}\hat{i} + g'(r) \frac{y}{r}\hat{j} = \frac{g'(r)}{r} (x\hat{i} + y\hat{j})\] We have to find \(g(r)\) such that \[\frac{g'(r)}{r} = r^n \Rightarrow g'(r) = r^{n+1}\] For \(n\neq 2\), \[g(r) = \frac{1}{n+2} r^{n+2}\] For \(n=2\), \[g(r) = \ln(r)\]
