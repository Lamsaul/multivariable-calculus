Problem 1

  1. Show that the vector field $$\vec{F}= (e^x y z)\hat{i} + (e^x z +2yz)\hat{j} + (e^xy + y^2 + 1)\hat{k}$$ is conservative.
  2. Find the potential of \(\vec{F}\).
  3. Show that the vector field $$\vec{G} = y\hat{i} + x\hat{j} + y\hat{z}$$ is not conservative.

Solution

  1. \[Curl(\vec{F}) = \begin{vmatrix} i & j & k \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\ e^x y z & e^x z +2yz & e^xy + y^2 + 1 \end{vmatrix} = \hat{i} \left(\frac{\partial(e^x y + y^2 + 1)}{\partial y} - \frac{\partial (e^x z + 2yz)}{\partial z}\right) - \hat{j} \left(\frac{\partial(e^x y + y^2 + 1)}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial (e^x yz)}{\partial z}\right) + \hat{k} \left(\frac{\partial(e^xz+2yz)}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial (e^x yz)}{\partial y}\right)\] \[\hat{i}(e^x+2y - e^x - 2y) - \hat{j}(e^x y - e^x y) + \hat{k}(e^x z - e^x z) = 0\]
  2. \[f = \int \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \cdot dx = \int e^x yz \cdot dx = e^x yz + g(y,z)\] \[\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = e^x z + g_y (y,z)\] \[e^x z + 2yz = e^xz + g_y(y,z) \Rightarrow g(y,z) = \int 2yz \cdot dy\] \[g(y,z) = y^2z + h(z) \Rightarrow \therefore f = e^xyz + y^2z + h(z)\] And we get \(h_z (z) = 1\), giving us \(h(z) = z+C\), \[\therefore f=e^xyz + y^2z + z+C\]
  3. \[Curl(G) = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\ y & x & y \end{vmatrix} = \hat{i}\left(1-0\right) - \hat{j}(0) + \hat{k}(1-1) = \hat{i}\] \(\therefore \vec{G}\) is not conservative.
