Problem 3

Let S be the part of the spherical surface \(x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 2\) lying in \(z > 1\). Orient \(S\) upwards and give its bounding circle, \(C\), lying in \(z = 1\) the compatible orientation.
  1. Parametrize \(C\) and use the parametrization to evaluate the line integral
    2. \[I = \oint_C xz\cdot dx + y \cdot dy + y \cdot dz\]
  2. Compute the curl of the vector field \(\textbf{F} = xz\hat{i} + y\hat{j} + y\hat{k}\)
  3. Write down a flux integral through \(S\) which can be computed using the value of \(I\).

Solution

  1. If \(z=1\), \(x^2+y^2=1\), so \[x=\cos{t} \Rightarrow dx = -dt\sin{t}\] \[y=\sin{t} \Rightarrow dy = dt\cos{t}\] \[z = 1 \Rightarrow dz = 0\] For \(0 \leq t \leq 2\pi\), \[\oint_C xz\cdot dx + y \cdot dy + y \cdot dz = \int_{t=0}^{t=2\pi} \cos{t} \cdot -dt\sin{t} + \sin{t} \cdot dt\cos{t} = \int_{t=0}^{t=2\pi} -\sin{t}\cos{t} + \sin{t}\cos{t} \cdot dt = 0 \]
  2. \[\nabla \times F = \begin{vmatrix} i & j & k \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\ xz & y & y \end{vmatrix} = \hat{i} \left(\frac{\partial y}{\partial y} - \frac{\partial y}{\partial z}\right) - \hat{j}\left(\frac{\partial y}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial xz}{\partial z}\right) + \hat{k}\left(\frac{\partial x}{\partial y}-\frac{\partial xz}{\partial y}\right)= \hat{i}+x\hat{j}\]
  3. By stokes' theorem, \[\oint_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = \int\int_S (\nabla \times F) \cdot \hat{n} \cdot dS\] Here, \(\hat{n}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(x, y, z)\), so, \[\int\int_S \frac{x+xy}{\sqrt{2}} \cdot dS\]
